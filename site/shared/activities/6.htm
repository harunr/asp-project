This full-day excursion takes you to Syracuse, one of the most beautiful towns in Sicily. The journey begins when you board a deluxe motorcoach with an English-speaking guide, stopping first at the Neapolis Archaeological Park. You will see the Roman Amphitheater, which was partly carved from rock during the Roman Imperial Age in the second century AD; and the Greek Theatre, whose cavea (seating section) is one of the largest ever built by the ancient Greeks. Carved out of solid rock and excellently preserved, the cavea has 67 rows divided into nine sections with eight aisles. The edifice, still used today, was modified by the Romans, who adapted it to circus games and other spectacles. Other highlights of the park include Grotta dei Cordari (Rope-makers Cave); and the Ear of Dionysius, an earlobe-shaped, artificial cave hand-carved 200 feet deep as a prison during the war with Athens.

Your next stop is Ortigia, a small island in the historical center of Syracuse. On a walking tour, you will discover the food and fish market; Pancali Square; the ruins of Apollo’s temple dating to the sixth century BC; Corso Matteotti, the city’s main and most elegant shopping area; and Archimede Square with the fountain of Artemis. Also, on the route are the Cathedral, which was originally the temple of Athena and converted into a Christian church in the seventh century; and the Church of Santa Lucia alla Badia, where the Caravaggio painting “Burial of St Lucy” is kept. After lunch at one of Syracuse’s most elegant restaurants, you will return to the motorcoach for the drive back to the hotel.
