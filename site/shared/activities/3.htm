A fascinating day begins with a drive to Catania aboard a comfortable minibus with an English-speaking guide. The splendid Baroque city and provincial capital lies on an extensive plain of citrus groves at the foot of Mount Etna next to the Ionian Sea. The city’s growth, for better or worse, has been affected by its relationship with the volcano. The eruptions made the fields remarkably fertile and, as the lava cooled, it became a quarry for the volcanic stone with which houses were built.

A walking tour will take you along Via Etnea, the main street and the longest in Europe, which is lined with shops and boutiques. After entering the Uzeda Gate, you will arrive at Cathedral Square, planned immediately after a 1693 earthquake in the same place as the medieval Platea Magna or main square, where you can admire the fountain of the elephant, the town symbol. All the buildings around the square were designed by the architect Vaccarini in Baroque style. Other highlights on the tour are the fish market; Via Crociferi, one of Catania’s most beautiful streets; Basicilica della Collegiata; the University of Catania; and Teatro Massimo Bellini (opera house). Along the way, you will enjoy a Sicilian cannolo with ricotta cheese.

At the end of the walking tour, you will board your minibus and drive along the coast through Sicilian villages including Aci Castello, which grew up around a Norman fortress. At Aci Trezza, a maritime town immortalized in the novel, “I Malavoglia,” by the Sicilian writer, Giovanni Verga, you will see the great shoals of basalt rock, or Faraglioni dei Ciclopi, rising from the water. After a brief visit, you will continue on to Taormina.
